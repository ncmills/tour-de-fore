Bandon Dunes sits on the southern Oregon coast, 85 miles from the nearest commercial airport and worlds away from resort golf. There are no carts, no GPS rangefinders on the handlebars, no condos bleeding into the rough. Just four links courses on 600-foot bluffs above the Pacific, and caddies who've probably walked more miles than you've driven.

## Why Bandon Deserves a Separate Trip

Most golf pilgrimages are built around a single course. You fly somewhere, play the famous track, squeeze in a B-list round, and leave. Bandon doesn't work that way. The four courses — Bandon Dunes, Pacific Dunes, Bandon Trails, and Old Macdonald — are credibly in the top 30 public courses in America, and each plays completely differently.

<DataHook>
  Golf Digest's 2024 ranking puts Pacific Dunes at No. 5 among all US public courses. Old Macdonald ranks No. 28. If you're flying to play one of them, you're flying past three others of the same caliber.
</DataHook>

Pacific Dunes was designed by Tom Doak and opens on a hole that runs straight toward the ocean. The green is visible from the tee. The wind is not predictable. By the third hole you understand what links golf actually means — not "seaside" golf, but golf where the ground matters as much as the air.

## How to Plan Four Rounds Without Destroying Yourself

The single worst thing you can do at Bandon is try to play 36 holes a day. It's walking-only, elevation changes are real, and the caddie loops are 4–5 hours. Four rounds in four days is plenty. Add a fifth round on day three only if your group has played links golf before.

<Callout type="note" title="Logistics tip">
  Lodge on-property. The drive from Coos Bay is beautiful and adds 40 minutes each way. On-property lodging means breakfast at 7am, on the first tee at 8am, and still done before happy hour.
</Callout>

A working three-day framework:

- **Day 1:** Bandon Dunes (the original, gentlest introduction to the property)
- **Day 2:** Pacific Dunes (the consensus best; play it fresh)
- **Day 3:** Old Macdonald in the morning, Bandon Trails in the afternoon if legs hold
- **Day 4:** Replay Pacific Dunes, or Sheep Ranch if your handicap clears the shot requirement

## What the Walk Actually Costs You

Bandon is expensive. Green fees run $265–$365 per round depending on season, and caddies are $100–$130 per bag plus tip. Budget $600–$700 per round, all-in, if you caddie every loop.

<KeyStat value="$2,400" label="all-in budget for four rounds with caddies" />

That number sounds large until you compare it to a private club initiation. Caddie fees are not optional at Bandon in any meaningful sense — the courses are too long and too exposed to navigate confidently without local knowledge on your first visit.

## FAQ

### Is Bandon Dunes worth the cost?

Yes, for serious golfers. The combination of course quality, walking culture, and caddie access is unlike anything else in American golf. If you play twice a year and one of those trips is Bandon, you're making the right trade-off.

### When is the best time to visit Bandon Dunes?

June through September offers the most consistent weather. July and August are peak season with the longest days. May and October are shoulder months — fewer crowds, similar conditions, and lower rates. Avoid winter unless you enjoy horizontal rain and 50mph gusts (some people do).
